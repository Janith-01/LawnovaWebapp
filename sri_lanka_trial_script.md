# ⚖️ Sri Lankan Mock Trial Dialogue Script
**Case Type:** Robbery with a Deadly Weapon (Section 380, Penal Code)
**Topic:** Criminal Law - The Use of Force in Theft

---

### **Part 1: The Prosecution's Case**
**JUDGE:** Court is now in session for the case of *Public Prosecutor vs. Jagath Mendis*. Mr. Prosecutor, call your first witness.

**PROSECUTOR:** I call the complainant, Mr. Nishantha Perera, to the witness box. Mr. Perera, please describe the evening of February 14th in Wellawatte.

**WITNESS:** I was walking home from the supermarket around 9:00 PM. Near the dark alley behind the temple, I felt someone grab my collar. A man, who I now recognize as the defendant, pulled out a large kitchen knife and pointed it at my throat.

**PROSECUTOR:** Did he speak?

**WITNESS:** Yes. He said, "Give me your phone and your wallet, or I will kill you here." He then snatched my bag, which had 25,000 rupees and my iPhone 14.

**PROSECUTOR:** Was there any physical struggle?

**WITNESS:** I was too afraid to struggle. He pushed me to the ground and ran away toward the main road. 

---

### **Part 2: The Cross-Examination (Defense Strategy)**
**JUDGE:** Defense, you may cross-examine.

**DEFENSE:** Mr. Perera, you said it was "dark." Isn't it true that visibility in that alley is less than five feet at night?

**WITNESS:** There was some light from the streetlamp at the corner.

**DEFENSE:** But you weren't wearing your prescription glasses that night, were you? You left them at the supermarket counter earlier that evening.

**WITNESS:** I... I can see well enough without them.

**DEFENSE:** The defendant was wearing a hoodie, correct? And a face mask?

**WITNESS:** Yes, but I saw his eyes.

**DEFENSE:** Your Honor, we contend that under **Section 380 of the Penal Code**, the prosecution must prove identity beyond a reasonable doubt. My client was elsewhere at the time.

---

### **Part 3: Legal Conclusions**
**PROSECUTOR:** Your Honor, the recovery of the iPhone from the defendant's house is sufficient evidence of robbery. Under the **Evidence Act, Section 114**, the court may presume that a person in possession of recently stolen goods is either the thief or has received the goods knowing them to be stolen.

**JUDGE:** I will take the evidence under advisement. Counsel, prepare your final arguments regarding the aggravating factor of the "deadly weapon" used under Section 380.
